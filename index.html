Project Overview

This project showcases a complete data cleaning and basic analysis workflow on a 40-record customer dataset. The original data contained realistic issues such as inconsistent capitalization, messy phone formats, email typos, mixed date formats, incomplete addresses, and missing membership information.

Using Microsoft Excel, I transformed the dataset into a clean, standardized version suitable for reporting and simple analytics, then generated insights using PivotTables.

Skills Demonstrated

Data Cleaning & Transformation

  • Standardized names using TRIM and PROPER to fix extra spaces and inconsistent casing.
  • Normalized phone numbers to a North American format: (XXX) XXX-XXXX.
  • Cleaned and validated email addresses, removed spaces, and fixed common domain issues.
  • Unified dates of birth to ISO format YYYY-MM-DD using SUBSTITUTE, DATEVALUE, and TEXT.
  • Normalized addresses and handled unknown or incomplete values in a consistent way.
  • Categorized membership status into standardized values: Yes, No, Unknown.

Analytical Preparation & Reporting

  • Created derived fields such as Birth Year and Email Domain using functions like YEAR and RIGHT / FIND.
  • Built PivotTables to summarize customers by membership status, age (birth year), and email domain.
  • Interpreted results and documented key findings in a structured report.

Key Insights & Takeaways

  • Member data gaps: A significant portion of customers are categorized as Unknown for membership status, suggesting that collection or entry processes could be improved.
  • Younger demographic: Most customers were born between 1997 and 2001, indicating a younger customer base that may respond well to digital-first services or campaigns.
  • Email providers: Gmail is the dominant email provider, followed by Hotmail and Live, which is useful for understanding communication channels and deliverability.
  • Data quality improvement: The project significantly improved the reliability of the dataset by standardizing formats, resolving inconsistencies, and handling missing values, making it suitable for further analysis or operational use.
